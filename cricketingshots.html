Famous Cricket Shots



1.The Helicopter Shot


     The shot got its name and fame through M.S.Dhoni, who played on a regular basis as a way to score boundaries against full and yorker length deliveries. It was a fellow player Santosh Lal, a childhood friend of Dhoni, who taught him how to play the shot.

     If there is anything more popular than MS Dhoni’s lightning quick stumpings, it is his trademark helicopter shot. Today, however, it was Hardik Pandya who, on the last ball of the Indian innings, managed to pull off an inch perfect helicopter shot that would have made Dhoni beam with pride.

2. Straight Drive


     In cricket, a straight drive is a deliberate shot aimed at hitting the ball straight along the ground to avoid being caught out.

     As far as the straight drive is concerned, Indian maestro Sachin Tendulkar might as well have patented the shot and prevented others from attempting to play it.

     However, what made him the best player of the shot was that he played it to perfection with far more regularity than most other batsmen and in addition to that, the straight bat and high elbows made the shot look even more spectacular.

3. Cover Drive


     The cover drive in cricket is the act of stroking the ball through the covers with well-timed wristwork and conventional movement of the front foot toward the pitch of a delivery aimed at or outside the off stump.

     Sachin Tendulkar, Virat Kohli, Babar Azam, are members of a select group of players who are widely considered as 'masters' of the shot with England’s Walter Hammond widely considered as having the finest cover drive ever.

4. Pull Shot


     The Pull Shot is an attacking stroke played to a delivery which pitches on a short length. Played generally off the back foot, the pull shot resembles the hook shot, but is often played around waist height. Unlike a hook, which is often played “off the nose”, a pull shot can actually be played to a delivery that is on off stump. Hence the name. The shot is “pulled” around to leg.

     Rohit Sharma, the master of Pull Shots. One of the greatest ever of world cricket.

5. The Scoop


     Tillakaratne Dilshan, a former Sri Lankan all-rounder, endorsed this stroke. He was the very initial player in history to execute the shot. The scoop shot was then used to counter quick bowlers and was considered a viable option.

     Jos Butler, Tillakaratne Dilshan, AB de Villiers Suryakumar Yadav members of a select group of players who are widely considered as 'masters' of the shot.
